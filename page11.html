Statistics of E-Commerce
Plastic usage has surged twentyfold in the last 50 years, with detrimental consequences when it turns into waste. Documentaries like Blue Planet II highlighting global plastic pollution impact have triggered public concern. A 2016 report by the Foundation revealed that the majority of plastic packaging is single-use, with only 14% recycled. Annually, USD 80-120 billion worth of plastic packaging, 95% of its value, is lost to the economy. Plastic production is highly energy-intensive, derived from fossil fuels like crude oil. In 2019, plastics contributed 1.8 billion metric tonnes of greenhouse gas emissions (3.4% of the global total). Packaging accounts for 36% of plastic use, with 85% of single-use food and beverage containers ending up in landfills. Of all plastic waste, 46% is landfilled, 22% becomes litter, causing environmental harm, as plastic does not biodegrade, affecting marine life, soil, and groundwater, with potential health impacts.
